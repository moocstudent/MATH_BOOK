## Core Notes

Many real situations come down to "how one quantity changes as another changes": distance grows with time, an electricity bill grows with usage, temperature falls with altitude. A **function** is the mathematical language for such a correspondence, and a **linear function** is the simplest and most common kind — its graph is a straight line. This chapter starts from "variables and correspondence", works out which two numbers fix the shape of that line, and finally connects it to equations and inequalities.

### Variables, functions and graphs

In a changing process, a quantity whose value changes is a **variable**; one whose value stays fixed is a **constant**.

**Definition of a function.** Suppose two variables $x$ and $y$ appear in some process. If for every allowed value of $x$ there is **exactly one** corresponding value of $y$, then $y$ is a **function** of $x$, written $y=f(x)$. Here $x$ is the **independent variable**, $y$ the **dependent variable**, and the set of allowed values of $x$ is the **domain**.

The crucial words are "exactly one": a single $x$ may not correspond to two different values of $y$. For instance $y=2x+1$ is a function; but "$y$ is a number whose absolute value equals $x$" is not, since $x=4$ allows $y=4$ or $y=-4$.

To *see* a function we use the **rectangular coordinate system**: two number lines meeting perpendicularly at a common origin — the horizontal $x$-axis and the vertical $y$-axis. Each point of the plane corresponds to an ordered pair $(a,b)$, its **coordinates**, with $a$ the $x$-coordinate and $b$ the $y$-coordinate. Plotting all points $(x,y)$ that satisfy $y=f(x)$ produces the **graph** of the function. The graph is a powerful tool — whether the function "rises or falls" and "which points it passes through" can be read off at a glance.

### Direct proportion

The simplest linear function is the **direct proportion**.

**Definition.** A function of the form
$$y=kx \quad (k\ne 0)$$
is a direct proportion, and the constant $k$ is the **constant of proportionality**. It expresses that two quantities are "directly proportional": multiplying $x$ by some factor multiplies $y$ by the same factor, and the ratio $\frac{y}{x}=k$ stays fixed.

**Graph and properties.** The graph of $y=kx$ is a straight line **through the origin $(0,0)$**. Since $x=1$ gives $y=k$, the line also passes through $(1,k)$, so joining the origin to $(1,k)$ draws it.

- When $k>0$, the graph lies in the first and third quadrants and $y$ **increases** as $x$ increases;
- When $k<0$, the graph lies in the second and fourth quadrants and $y$ **decreases** as $x$ increases.

The larger $|k|$ is, the "steeper" the line, so $k$ is also called the **slope**: it measures how much $y$ changes when $x$ increases by $1$.

### Graph and properties (slope and intercept)

Shifting a direct proportion bodily up or down gives the general linear function.

**Definition.** A function of the form
$$y=kx+b \quad (k\ne 0)$$
is a **linear function**, with constants $k$ and $b$. When $b=0$ it reduces to a direct proportion, so direct proportion is a special case.

The graph of a linear function is again a **straight line**, and the two constants play distinct roles:

- **$k$ controls the direction (increase/decrease).** As before, $k>0$ makes the line rise from lower-left to upper-right ($y$ increases with $x$); $k<0$ makes it fall from upper-left to lower-right. Larger $|k|$ means steeper.

{{viz:linearK}}

- **$b$ controls the intercept (vertical position).** Setting $x=0$ gives $y=b$, so the line meets the $y$-axis at $(0,b)$; this $b$ is the **$y$-intercept**. The graph of $y=kx+b$ is the graph of $y=kx$ shifted up ($b>0$) or down ($b<0$) by $|b|$ units along the $y$-axis.

{{viz:linearB}}

Since "two points determine a line", drawing a linear function needs only two points. The handiest are the two **intercept points**: the $y$-axis point $(0,b)$, and the $x$-axis point $\left(-\frac{b}{k},\,0\right)$ found by setting $y=0$ and solving $x=-\frac{b}{k}$.

### Finding the formula

Conversely, given the graph (or some conditions), we recover the formula by the **method of undetermined coefficients**.

**Idea.** A linear function has the fixed template $y=kx+b$ with two unknown constants $k$ and $b$. Find two independent conditions (usually two known points on the graph), substitute their coordinates to get a **system of two linear equations** in $k$ and $b$, and solve.

For example, if a line passes through $(1,3)$ and $(2,5)$, substitution gives
$$\begin{cases} k+b=3,\\ 2k+b=5. \end{cases}$$
Subtracting yields $k=2$, and back-substitution gives $b=1$, so the formula is $y=2x+1$. The general principle: *as many unknown coefficients, so many conditions needed.*

### Links to equations and inequalities

A linear function turns algebraic objects — equations and inequalities — into geometry; the three are different faces of one thing.

**Linear equation in one unknown.** Solving $kx+b=0$ asks "for which $x$ does $y=kx+b$ equal zero", i.e. the $x$-coordinate where the graph meets the $x$-axis. So the solution of $kx+b=0$ is exactly the $x$-value of the intersection of the line $y=kx+b$ with the $x$-axis.

**Linear inequality in one unknown.** The solution set of $kx+b>0$ is the range of $x$ for which the graph lies **above** the $x$-axis ($y>0$); $kx+b<0$ corresponds to the part of the graph **below** the $x$-axis.

**System of two linear equations.** The **intersection** of two lines $y=k_1x+b_1$ and $y=k_2x+b_2$ has coordinates satisfying both formulas at once, so it is precisely the solution of the system
$$\begin{cases} y=k_1x+b_1,\\ y=k_2x+b_2. \end{cases}$$
"Solving the system" and "finding where two lines cross" are the same task.

### Summary

- Function: each input $x$ gives **exactly one** $y$, written $y=f(x)$; graph it in the rectangular coordinate system, points $(x,y)$.
- Direct proportion $y=kx\,(k\ne0)$: a line through the origin and $(1,k)$; rising if $k>0$, falling if $k<0$.
- Linear function $y=kx+b\,(k\ne0)$: a line; $k$ sets direction and steepness, $b$ sets the $y$-axis point $(0,b)$; meets the $x$-axis at $\left(-\frac{b}{k},0\right)$.
- Undetermined coefficients: set $y=kx+b$, substitute two known points, solve for $k,b$.
- Links: the solution of $kx+b=0$ is the $x$-intercept; the solution of $kx+b>0$ is where the graph is above the $x$-axis; the intersection of two lines is the solution of the corresponding system.

## Worked Examples

**Example 1.** Given that $y$ is a direct proportion of $x$ and that $y=-6$ when $x=3$, find the formula and state whether $y$ increases or decreases as $x$ increases.

> **Solution.** Set $y=kx$. Substituting $x=3,\,y=-6$: $-6=3k$, so $k=-2$. The formula is
> $$y=-2x.$$
> Since $k=-2<0$, $y$ **decreases** as $x$ increases, and the graph lies in the second and fourth quadrants.

**Example 2.** A linear function passes through $(1,1)$ and $(3,-3)$. Find its formula.

> **Solution.** Use undetermined coefficients. Set $y=kx+b$ and substitute both points:
> $$\begin{cases} k+b=1,\\ 3k+b=-3. \end{cases}$$
> Subtracting gives $2k=-4$, so $k=-2$; back into $k+b=1$ gives $b=3$. Hence
> $$y=-2x+3.$$

**Example 3.** For the linear function $y=2x-4$, find where its graph meets the $x$-axis and the $y$-axis, and state the values of $x$ for which $y>0$.

> **Solution.** $y$-axis point: set $x=0$, giving $y=-4$, so $(0,-4)$.
> $x$-axis point: set $y=0$, giving $2x-4=0$, i.e. $x=2$, so $(2,0)$.
> Since $k=2>0$, the graph rises from lower-left to upper-right and lies above the $x$-axis only to the **right** of $(2,0)$. Thus $y>0$ means $2x-4>0$, giving
> $$x>2.$$

**Example 4.** A taxi charges a base fare of \$8 (for up to 3 km) and \$2 per km beyond 3 km. Let the trip be $x$ km ($x\ge 3$) and the fare $y$ dollars. Find $y$ as a function of $x$, and the fare for a 10 km trip.

> **Solution.** For $x\ge 3$, the distance beyond 3 km is $(x-3)$ km at \$2 each, plus the \$8 base:
> $$y=8+2(x-3)=2x+2 \quad (x\ge 3).$$
> This is a linear function. When $x=10$, $y=2\times 10+2=22$, so the fare is \$22.

**Example 5.** Solve the system $\begin{cases} y=x+1,\\ y=-x+3 \end{cases}$ graphically and explain the geometric meaning.

> **Solution.** Both equations are linear functions. Find the intersection by setting them equal:
> $$x+1=-x+3 \;\Rightarrow\; 2x=2 \;\Rightarrow\; x=1.$$
> Substituting into $y=x+1$ gives $y=2$. So the solution of the system is
> $$\begin{cases} x=1,\\ y=2. \end{cases}$$
> Geometric meaning: the lines $y=x+1$ and $y=-x+3$ cross at $(1,2)$, whose coordinates satisfy both equations at once — hence it is the solution of the system.

## Exercises

1. Decide whether each correspondence is a function: (a) $y=3x-2$; (b) $y^2=x$ (with $y$ as a function of $x$).
2. The graph of the direct proportion $y=kx$ passes through $(-2,6)$. Find $k$ and state which quadrants the graph lies in.
3. Find where the graph of $y=-\frac{1}{2}x+3$ meets the two coordinate axes.
4. A linear function passes through $(0,-1)$ and $(2,3)$. Find its formula.
5. For $y=-3x+6$, solve the inequality $-3x+6\ge 0$ (explain using the $x$-intercept of the graph).
6. For a linear function $y=kx+b$, $y=4$ when $x=1$ and $y=0$ when $x=-1$. Find $k$ and $b$, and state whether $y$ increases or decreases.

**Answers.** 1) (a) is a function (each $x$ gives one $y$); (b) is not — e.g. $x=4$ gives $y=\pm 2$, two values for one $x$; 2) substituting, $6=-2k$ so $k=-3$; since $k<0$ the graph lies in the second and fourth quadrants; 3) $y$-axis at $(0,3)$ (set $x=0$), $x$-axis at $(6,0)$ (set $y=0$, giving $x=6$); 4) set $y=kx+b$; from $(0,-1)$, $b=-1$; from $(2,3)$, $2k+b=3$, so $2k-1=3$, $k=2$, giving $y=2x-1$; 5) setting $y=0$ gives $x=2$; since $k=-3<0$ the graph is above the $x$-axis only left of $(2,0)$, so $-3x+6\ge0$ has solution $x\le 2$; 6) from $\begin{cases}k+b=4\\-k+b=0\end{cases}$, adding gives $2b=4$, $b=2$, then $k=2$; since $k>0$, $y$ increases as $x$ increases.
